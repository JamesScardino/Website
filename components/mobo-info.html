All About Motherboards

What is the motherboard? The motherboard is a computer's central communications backbone connectivity point, through which all components and external peripherals connect.

Main Functions of a Motherboard

  • Houses the CPU and facilitates its communication with other components.
  • Hosts the RAM, which is crucial for running applications and the operating system.
  • Provides expansion slots for additional cards, such as graphics cards and sound cards.
  • Contains connectors for storage devices like SSDs and HDDs.
  • Includes power connectors to distribute power from the PSU to other components.

Types of Motherboards

  • ATX: Standard size with multiple expansion slots. Ideal for most builds.
  • Micro-ATX: Smaller, with fewer expansion slots. Suitable for mid-sized builds.
  • Mini-ITX: Compact, with limited slots. Perfect for small form factor builds.

Key Features to Consider

  • CPU Socket: Must be compatible with your chosen CPU.
  • RAM Slots: Ensure enough slots for your memory needs.
  • Expansion Slots: Look for the required number and type of slots for additional cards.
  • Storage Connectors: Check for the availability of SATA and M.2 slots for your drives.
  • I/O Ports: Ensure there are enough USB, audio, and video ports for your peripherals.
