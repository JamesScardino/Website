All About Case Fans

Case fans are an essential component for maintaining optimal airflow and cooling within a computer case. They help dissipate heat generated by other components, such as the CPU and GPU, to keep the system running smoothly.

There are different types of case fans available, including:

  • Intake Fans: These fans draw cool air into the case from the outside.
  • Exhaust Fans: These fans expel warm air from inside the case to the outside.

Choosing the right case fans involves considering factors like size, airflow (measured in CFM), and noise level (measured in dBA). Proper placement and configuration of case fans can significantly improve the cooling efficiency of your system.

Maintaining a good balance of intake and exhaust fans is crucial for creating a positive or neutral airflow, which helps prevent dust buildup and ensures efficient cooling.
