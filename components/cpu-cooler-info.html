All About CPU Coolers

A CPU cooler is a device designed to dissipate heat away from the CPU to prevent it from overheating. It consists of a heatsink and a fan or multiple fans.

There are several types of CPU coolers available:

  • Air CPU coolers: These use a combination of metal heatsinks and fans to cool the CPU.
  • Liquid CPU coolers: These use a closed-loop system containing coolant to transfer heat away from the CPU to a radiator, where it is dissipated by fans. These also come in a variety of sizes for whichever best fits your case size/needs.
  • Low-profile CPU coolers: These are designed for small form factor (SFF) PCs where space is limited.

The choice of CPU cooler depends on factors such as CPU compatibility, case size, cooling performance, and noise level.

Proper cooling is essential for maintaining CPU performance and longevity, especially during demanding tasks such as gaming or video editing.
